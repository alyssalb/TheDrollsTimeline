English Theatre from the Elizabethan Era to the Stuart Restoration

1558 – Elizabeth I is Crowned

Start of the Elizabethan era, a time of great patronage and support for the arts. Theatre flourished with royal and noble support both monetarily and affectively.

1576 – The Theatre Opens in Shoreditch

The first permanent purpose-built public playhouse in London opens, built by James Burbage. It launched a new era of professional acting companies and single-purpose venues. The Theatre was later dismantled, and its materials used to construct The Globe in 1599.
